Diffie-Hellman

The Diffie-Hellman-algorithm is a way to exchange a key securely through an unsecure channel.

The two parties which want to communicate agree on a multiplicative group modulo the prime p and a generator g of this group. Party A chooses a random value X_a and computes Y_a = g^(X_a) mod p.
The same does party B: Y_b = g^(X_b) mod p. Y_a and Y_b are the public keys of A and B and are sent to the other party.

Now A can compute the exchanged key as K = Y_b^X_a mod p. B gets the same key by computing K = Y_a^X_b mod p. An adversary not knowing X_a or X_b has no simple possibility to compute K.
